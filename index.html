Editor's Guidelines

Editor's Field Guide

Who vs. Whom (The "He/Him" Trick)

Replace the word with "He" or "Him". If He fits, use Who. If Him fits, use Whom.

"To whom (him) did you give it?"

Affect vs. Effect (RAVEN)

Remember: Affect is a Verb (Action). Effect is a Noun (Result).

Fewer vs. Less

Use Fewer for things you can count (cookies, people). Use Less for things you can't (water, time).

Lie vs. Lay

You lie down yourself, but you lay an object down.

It's vs. Its (The "Is" Test)

Read the sentence as "It Is". If it makes sense, use It's. Otherwise, use Its for ownership.

Semicolons (;)

Use a semicolon to connect two complete, related sentences. It's like a "super comma".

"It was raining; we stayed inside."

Comma Splice

You cannot join two complete sentences with only a comma. This is a comma splice.

"He is fast, he is strong."

Fix: "He is fast, and he is strong." or "He is fast; he is strong."

Dangling Modifiers

A descriptive phrase must be immediately followed by the thing it's describing.

"Running to the store, the rain fell." (The rain can't run).

Fix: "Running to the store, I got wet."

Passive Voice (The "Zombie" Test)

If you can add "by zombies" after the verb, it's passive voice. Active is stronger.

"The ball was thrown (by zombies)." → Passive.

"The boy threw the ball." → Active.

Parallelism

Items in a list must have the same grammatical form.

"I like hiking, swimming, and to run."

Fix: "I like hiking, swimming, and running."
