Editor's Field Guide

Word Choice & Confusing Pairs

Who vs. Whom (The "He/Him" Trick)

Replace the word with "He" or "Him".

  • If you can say He, use Who. (Both end in vowels).
  • If you can say Him, use Whom. (Both end in M).

"To whom (him) did you give it?"

Affect vs. Effect (RAVEN)

Remember: Affect is a Verb (Action). Effect is a Noun (Result).

"The weather affected (changed) the crops." vs "The special effects were cool."

Fewer vs. Less (The "Count" Rule)
  • Fewer: Things you can count on fingers (Cookies, people, dollars).
  • Less: Things you can't count individually (Water, money, sand, time).
Lie vs. Lay
  • Lie: To recline yourself. (No object). "I need to lie down."
  • Lay: To place something else. (Needs object). "Lay the book down."

Punctuation Rules

It's vs. Its (The "Is" Test)

Read the sentence as "It Is". If it makes sense, use the apostrophe.

  • It's: "It's (It is) raining."
  • Its: "The dog chased its tail." (Ownership, like 'His').
Semicolons (;) The "Super Comma"

Use a semicolon to connect two complete sentences that are related.

"It was raining; we stayed inside." (Both sides could stand alone).

Comma Splice (The "Weak Link")

You cannot join two sentences with just a comma. It's too weak.

"He is fast, he is strong."

"He is fast, and he is strong." or "He is fast; he is strong."

Sentence Structure

Dangling Modifiers (The "Who" Rule)

The descriptive phrase at the start must be immediately followed by the person/thing doing the action.

"Running to the store, the rain fell." (The rain can't run).

"Running to the store, I got wet."

Passive Voice (The "Zombie" Test)

If you can add "by zombies" after the verb, it's passive. Active voice is usually stronger.

"The ball was thrown (by zombies)." -> Passive.

"The boy threw the ball." -> Active.

Parallelism (The "Matching Game")

Items in a list must follow the same pattern (all -ing, all nouns, etc).

"I like hiking, swimming, and to run."

"I like hiking, swimming, and running."
